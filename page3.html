Transitional Work

Multimedia Projects

Audio

The effective use of sound requires intelligibility in recordings of the human voice as well as a creative exploration of how reality can be communicated to audiences beyond what's spoken.

When considering the life of audio in films, one has to ask themselves: Will your project remain exclusively in a realistic sound design or will there be moments of subjective or expressionistic sound? Will your documentary use music and if so, how?

These questions open up a world that we can explore simply through the power of listening and hearing sounds. One thing I will always remember from my classes in media practices is that a video with good audio and poor visuals will always be more well recieved than one with poor audio and decent visuals.

Acoustic Radio Interference

This audio was my final project for Sound and Technology, in which I applied as much content and techniques of capturing and editing sounds I gained from the class to create a rhythmic exploration of radio interferences and live recorded music.

I captured most of the audio for this project myself, using a handheld radio for the static noise and live stations, and an H6 audio recorder to record me playing a few of my favorite songs on the guitar. I did, however, download the actual songs to give the effect that they were being played on the radio but were constantly intterupted by my own acoustic covers.

Saturday Night at Union Station

This was an audio project for my Creative Media Making class, where I pushed myself to talk to strangers at Union Station, capturing some interesting interactions.

All of the audio used in this project was captured in one night using the same old H6 recorder and an NGT-2 microphone. I edited the sounds in a manner that would tell a story in a kind of immersive way. Here I employed my narration as a way to provide some context for the responses my subjects provided, without including very much of my actual presence in the space.

The project was inspired by the format of the Phantom Power podcasts.

Photography & Design

Making strong preparatory choices about how to frame and light shots as well as deciding whether the camera will be on a tripod or handheld is vital to producing effective visual work.

Learning how to operate a camera has been a process that I am still navigating today as I continue to explore and try out different gear out of mere curiosity. Over time I have also had the opportunity to aquire my own video camera and a digital photo camera, and even though they're not of the greatest quality, they provide depictions of real life. Understranding the various components of these devices can only be beneficial as we attempt to capture the right moments in the best frames possible. As a photographer and/or cinematographer, we are in control of shooting formats, lighting, shot size, camera placement, and so much more.
